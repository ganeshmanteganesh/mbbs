*   **Object1: Complement System**
    *   **Concept:** A crucial component of the innate immune system.

*   **Object2: Definition & Role**
    *   **Concept:** A system of ~50 plasma proteins that "complements" or enhances the adaptive immune response (antibodies) and phagocytic cells (e.g., macrophages, neutrophils). Its primary roles are to eliminate microbes, clear damaged/apoptotic cells, promote inflammation, and directly lyse pathogens. It functions as a tightly regulated biochemical cascade to prevent host tissue damage.

*   **Object3: Activation Pathways**
    *   **Concept:** Three distinct mechanisms initiate the complement cascade:
        *   **Classical pathway:** Triggered primarily by antibody-antigen complexes (e.g., IgG, IgM binding to pathogens) or C-reactive protein.
        *   **Alternative pathway:** Activated directly by microbial surfaces (e.g., bacterial cell walls, fungal polysaccharides) in the absence of antibodies.
        *   **Lectin pathway:** Initiated when mannose-binding lectin (MBL) or ficolins bind to specific carbohydrate patterns (e.g., mannose, N-acetylglucosamine) on microbial surfaces.

*   **Object4: Key Functions/Outcomes**
    *   **Concept:** The diverse biological effects of complement activation:
        *   **Opsonization:** Coating of pathogens by complement proteins (especially C3b) to enhance their recognition and phagocytosis by immune cells.
        *   **Chemotaxis:** Recruitment of phagocytes and other immune cells to sites of infection or inflammation via complement fragments (e.g., C5a).
        *   **Cell Lysis:** Direct destruction of target cells (e.g., bacteria, infected cells) through the formation of the Membrane Attack Complex (MAC), which punches holes in cell membranes.
        *   **Inflammation:** Promotion of local inflammatory responses through the release of anaphylatoxins (C3a, C5a), which cause vasodilation, increased vascular permeability, and mast cell degranulation (releasing histamine).
        *   **Clearance:** Removal of immune complexes (antibody-antigen aggregates) and apoptotic cellular debris from circulation.

*   **Object5: Clinical Relevance**
    *   **Concept:** The impact of complement dysregulation and its therapeutic implications:
        *   **Deficiencies:** Genetic defects in complement proteins can lead to increased susceptibility to certain infections (e.g., *Neisseria* species with MAC component deficiencies) or autoimmune diseases.
        *   **Overactivity:** Uncontrolled or inappropriate complement activation can contribute to autoimmune conditions (e.g., lupus nephritis due to impaired immune complex clearance, atypical hemolytic uremic syndrome from dysregulation of alternative pathway) and inflammatory diseases.
        *   **Therapeutic Targets:** Specific complement proteins are targets for therapeutic intervention. For example, eculizumab is an antibody that inhibits C5, used to treat paroxysmal nocturnal hemoglobinuria (PNH) and atypical hemolytic uremic syndrome.
