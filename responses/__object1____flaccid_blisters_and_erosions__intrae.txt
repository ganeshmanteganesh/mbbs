**Object1: Pemphigus Vulgaris**

*   **Clinical Features:** Characterized by **flaccid, easily ruptured intraepidermal blisters and erosions**. A **positive Nikolsky's sign** is present (epidermal detachment with lateral pressure). Lesions often begin in the **oral mucosa** and can become widespread, causing severe pain and being potentially life-threatening due to fluid loss, infection, and protein wasting.
*   **Pathophysiology/Significance:** An **autoimmune blistering disease** caused by autoantibodies (IgG) targeting desmoglein 1 and 3 (components of desmosomes, which provide cell-to-cell adhesion in the epidermis). This leads to **acantholysis** (loss of keratinocyte cohesion), resulting in superficial, fragile blisters.

**Object2: Bullous Pemphigoid**

*   **Clinical Features:** Presents with **tense, subepidermal blisters** that are resistant to rupture, often arising from an urticarial (hive-like) base. A **negative Nikolsky's sign** is typical. Intense **pruritus** (itching) is common. It frequently affects the **elderly** and favors **flexural areas** (e.g., armpits, groin).
*   **Pathophysiology/Significance:** An **autoimmune blistering disease** caused by autoantibodies (IgG and/or IgE) targeting hemidesmosomal proteins (BP180 and BP230), which are crucial for keratinocyte adhesion to the basement membrane. This leads to a deeper, more robust split below the epidermis, creating tense blisters.

**Object3: Dermatitis Herpetiformis**

*   **Clinical Features:** Manifests as intensely **pruritic papules, vesicles, and bullae** that typically appear in **grouped, symmetrical patterns** (herpetiform). Favors characteristic locations like the **elbows, knees, buttocks, and scalp**.
*   **Pathophysiology/Significance:** A specific **cutaneous manifestation of celiac disease**, strongly associated with **gluten-sensitive enteropathy**. It is an autoimmune condition mediated by IgA antibodies that deposit in the dermal papillae, targeting epidermal transglutaminase 3 (eTG). Histologically, it shows subepidermal vesicles with neutrophils in the dermal papillae. Management requires a strict **gluten-free diet**.
